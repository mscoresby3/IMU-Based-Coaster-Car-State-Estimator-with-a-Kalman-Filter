Accelerometer 
Measures: Proper acceleration, linear motion, and changes in direction.
Usage: Detecting shaking, tilting, and step counting (like in smartphones for portrait/landscape detection).
Limitation: Less effective at detecting fast, complex rotations.
Detectes non-gravity acceleration.

Gyroscope
Measures: Angular velocity (rotation speed around an axis).
Usage: Detecting orientation, spinning, and precise gestures (like in VR headsets or controlling a camera).
Limitation: Susceptible to "drift," meaning long-term accuracy can degrade without correction.
Uses gravity to determine orientation.

Key Differences
Motion Type: Accelerometer handles linear motion; Gyroscope handles rotational motion.
Gravity: Accelerometers are affected by gravity, allowing them to determine "down". Gyroscopes are not affected by gravity.
Application: Both are used together for high-precision navigation, robotics, and smartphones to provide a complete picture of movement.


When they are used together it is called an IMU (integrated motion unit).
